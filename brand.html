Logo

The Mark

White on Cobalt
Cobalt on Cream
White on Navy

The mark. Two glyphs in one: a four-rayed sunburst rising over a single horizontal wave. Sun over water — the literal Sunshine Lake.

Clearspace. Keep a minimum margin around the mark equal to the height of the wave glyph on all four sides.

Don’t. Recolor outside the brand palette, rotate, stretch, or set on busy photography without a scrim.

04

Visual Motif

Painterly Water

The Surface Sunshine Yellow caustics over a deep cobalt lake.

The hero on every Sunshine Lake surface — site, deck, cover slides — is the painterly water: cobalt depth with sunshine-yellow caustics rippling across it. It’s rendered in WebGL on the site (a fragment shader), but reproduces faithfully as a static gradient with radial yellow highlights.

Use it for: covers, hero sections, divider plates. Don’t use it as background behind body copy.

05

Voice

Five principles

Conviction, not consensus.

We back what others miss. Our copy speaks from a clear point of view — never the marketing-passive "may be considered" voice.

Spare. Editorial.

Short sentences. Period. Long ones earn their place by carrying weight. Domaine display does the talking — keep flourish to a minimum.

Concrete over abstract.

"19x liquid TVPI." "First check into Deel." Specifics build trust faster than adjectives. Numbers, names, places.

One italic at a time.

The italicized rhetorical beat ("What better time to build…") is the brand’s exhale. Use it once per surface, at the end.

Beneath the surface.

The thesis through-line. Our work is the unseen craft — the rigor under the visible result. Echo it in voice without overusing the phrase.
